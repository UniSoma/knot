# Coupling scores a ticket by its live-induced undirected `:deps`+`:links` degree

The three *live* listing commands (`list`, `ready`, `blocked`) gain a **`CPL`** column and a `coupling` integer in `--json`. Coupling is the count of *distinct live tickets* a row is directly connected to through `:deps` (in either direction) or `:links` — its undirected 1-hop degree over those two axes, computed on the **live-induced** graph, `:parent` deliberately excluded. It is always computed and always shown; in v1 it never sorts, never gates, and never appears on `closed`. The number answers one question: *how much surrounding live context must I hold to reason about this ticket?* High coupling marks where complexity concentrates in the open backlog, so reading the column is a diagnostic heatmap — the eye is drawn to the tangled rows, which are the de-entanglement targets. It serves *cognitive-load reduction*: `--closure` is computed on demand but gives no passive, at-a-glance sense of *where* the entanglement is; coupling is that always-on signal.

Three properties define the metric, each a deliberate cut against a plausible alternative. **A per-row degree (a gradient), not articulation points (a flag).** The goal is to *see where complexity is* — a diagnostic value on every row — not to mark the few structural cut-points. Articulation points answer "where do I cleave?", a sparse prescriptive flag that conveys no sense of the distribution; they are the v2 companion that acts on the hot-spots this column reveals. **`:deps` ∪ `:links`, undirected, `:parent` excluded** — and this is the precise *inverse* of leverage's axis choice. For a comprehension question every relationship a reader must load is signal, so `:links` (a see-also) *counts* here even though it is noise for leverage's directed "waiting" question — the same edge, opposite role, because the questions are opposite. `:parent` is excluded because an undirected parent edge inflates an *umbrella's* degree by its child count, recasting organized hierarchy as a false complexity hot-spot — and that rollup is already carried by Umbrella progress (`CHLD`). Direction is dropped because cognitive load runs both ways: what a ticket waits on and what waits on it are equally context to hold. **A plain 1-hop count, not local clustering or a weighted blend.** Degree is legible; the truer "snarl" measure (how densely a ticket's neighbors interconnect) is an opaque float and is deferred — degree is *star-blind* (a clean hub scores like a tangle), accepted for v1 and revisited only if shown to mislead.

The crux is the **live-induced** scope, shared with leverage and against closure. A dep on a *closed* ticket is resolved — no longer an open concern you juggle, so it adds no *current* load; a ticket whose neighbors are all closed is untangled *now*, and its historical complexity is not something you can act on (a closed ticket cannot be de-tangled — it is done). Counting only live neighbors keeps coupling equal to the entanglement of the *current* backlog, which is the only complexity the column can prompt you to reduce. This places coupling at a distinct third point among the graph views: it shares **live-induced** scope with leverage (both are current-work columns) but shares the **undirected, multi-axis** shape of closure (both are comprehension/relatedness) — yet it is neither, because its edges follow the cognitive-load question specifically (links in, parent out).

The surface is intentionally minimal and mirrors leverage: no flag, no sort, an always-on column, an always-on `--json` field. Always-on JSON does not reopen ADR 0010's rejection of `--json` pollution — that targeted per-*query* metadata (`closure_via`/`closure_distance`, annotations explaining why a row matched *this* filter). Coupling is an **intrinsic property of a live ticket given the corpus**, as query-independent as `leverage` or `priority`: a row's live neighbor-count is the same whether you ran `list`, `list --tag x`, or `ready`. The field is additive and safe (consumers ignore unknown keys), and it is what keeps the deferred downstream surfaces — knot.el heat-coloring, the v2 articulation-point flag — reachable without re-introducing a flag.

## Considered options

- **Articulation points / cut vertices as the v1 metric** — rejected for v1. A sparse, prescriptive flag answers "where do I cleave?", not "where *is* the complexity"; it conveys no sense of the distribution across the list. Deferred to v2 as the prescriptive companion that acts on the hot-spots the coupling column surfaces.
- **All-axes degree, including `:parent`** — rejected. An undirected `:parent` edge inflates an umbrella's degree by its child count, recasting organized hierarchy as a complexity hot-spot, and duplicates the `CHLD` / Umbrella-progress rollup. Excluding `:parent` isolates cross-cutting entanglement from intended composition.
- **`:deps`-only, excluding `:links`** — rejected. For a cognitive-load question a see-also is genuine context a reader must load; the edge that is noise for leverage's directed "waiting" question is signal here. The edge set follows the question — the inverse of ADR 0011's choice — not a convention.
- **Directed degree** — rejected. Cognitive load runs both directions: what a ticket waits on and what waits on it are equally context to hold. Direction encodes "who waits for whom," which is leverage's concern, not comprehension's.
- **Betweenness / other path-based centrality** — rejected. Opaque normalized floats; a ranking nobody can explain. Coupling is a plain, explainable 1-hop count.
- **Local clustering coefficient / neighbor-density** — deferred to v2. Truer to "snarl" than degree (which is star-blind — a clean hub scores like a tangle), but an opaque [0,1] float. Count first; refine only if the plain count is shown to mislead.
- **Full-corpus computation (mirroring `closure-set`)** — rejected. Closed neighbors are resolved work, not current cognitive load, and a closed ticket cannot be de-tangled; counting them would mark historical complexity the column can't prompt action on. Faithfulness for a current-load question requires the live-induced graph — the same corpus-scope logic as leverage, against closure.
- **Standalone `knot coupling` command** — rejected, per ADR 0010's precedent. A column composes for free with `--type` / `--tag` / `--closure` / `--limit`; a separate command would re-implement that composition.
- **`--coupling` opt-in flag / `--sort coupling`** — rejected/deferred. The number is intrinsic and cheap (1-hop, the corpus is already in memory); gating it would starve the downstream coloring surfaces. A sort grammar is speculative v1 scope — present the number, rank by eye.
- **Coupling on `closed`** — rejected. A closed ticket's current load is ~0 by construction; an all-zero column is noise.

## Consequences

- A new primitive lands in `query.clj`: it builds the undirected `:deps` ∪ `:links` adjacency *restricted to live tickets* — the union of forward-`:deps`, reverse-`:deps` (via the existing `inverses`), and `:links` — and counts the distinct live neighbors of each row. It reuses `closure-set`'s adjacency machinery axis-filtered to `deps`+`links`, drops broken refs, and is cheaper than leverage (1-hop, no transitive walk, no cycle guard needed).
- `list`, `ready`, and `blocked` render a `CPL` column and add a `coupling` integer to `--json`. `closed`, `show`, `dep tree`, `delete`, `check` are untouched. Default human output gains one narrow column; default JSON gains one additive, intrinsic field.
- `CONTEXT.md` gains a **Coupling** glossary entry under "Ticket relationships," with `_Avoid_` steering away from "centrality"/"betweenness," "articulation point," "complexity," "leverage," and "closure."
- v2 surfaces are additive and need not revisit this ADR: an **articulation-point flag** (the prescriptive cut-finder), a **local-clustering** refinement for star-blindness, `--sort coupling`, and knot.el row heat-coloring — all consume the `coupling` JSON field this ADR establishes.
- Per the AGENTS.md skill-sync rule, `.claude/skills/knot/SKILL.md` must document the `CPL` column and the `coupling` JSON field in the same commit that ships them.
